# CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md

## Code of Conduct

TheEye aims to be a respectful, constructive, and technically serious project.

Everyone participating in the project is expected to contribute in a way that supports collaboration, clarity, and mutual respect.

---

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- communicate respectfully
- give technical feedback constructively
- focus criticism on ideas, code, and decisions rather than people
- respect differences in experience level
- keep discussions relevant to the project
- assume good intent unless there is clear reason not to

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- harassment or intimidation
- personal attacks or insults
- discriminatory language or conduct
- bad-faith disruption of discussion or review
- repeated hostile or unconstructive behavior
- publishing private information without permission

---

## Technical Collaboration Standard

Because TheEye uses a review-oriented engineering workflow, contributors should also avoid:

- intentionally bypassing documented process
- pushing unrelated scope into focused changes
- creating unnecessary conflict in reviews
- misrepresenting test status or readiness

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions and participation that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

Responses may include:

- a warning
- request for behavior change
- rejection of a contribution
- temporary or permanent removal from participation spaces

---

## Reporting

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the project maintainer through an appropriate private contact channel.

Public callouts should be avoided unless immediate safety requires it.

---

## Final Note

The goal of this Code of Conduct is not formality for its own sake.
It exists to protect productive collaboration and keep the project healthy.
